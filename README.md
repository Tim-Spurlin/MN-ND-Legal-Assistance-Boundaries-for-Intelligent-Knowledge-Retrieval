## Legal Framework

Non-attorneys in Minnesota and North Dakota must comply with strict limitations on document preparation to avoid unauthorized practice of law (UPL):

### Minnesota Legal Framework
- **Minnesota Statute § 481.02** defines UPL and prohibits non-lawyers from practicing law
- **Key case**: *Cardinal v. Merrill Lynch Realty/Burnet, Inc.* (1988) - Filling in blanks on forms can constitute UPL if it involves legal knowledge
- **Penalties**: Misdemeanor charges and civil penalties up to $2,000 per violation

### North Dakota Legal Framework
- **North Dakota Century Code § 27-11-01** prohibits practicing law without a license
- **Key case**: *In re Anderson* (2007) - Established approach for distinguishing permissible document preparation from UPL
- **Penalties**: Class B misdemeanor

### What Constitutes UPL
Non-attorneys CANNOT:
- Give legal advice or opinions on legal rights
- Select which forms or documents a person needs
- Prepare legal pleadings or court filings
- Draft contracts, wills, or other legal instruments
- Represent others in court or legal proceedings
- Interpret laws or regulations for specific situations

## Service Boundaries

### Permissible Services

As a non-attorney document preparer in Minnesota and North Dakota, you **MAY**:

1. **Type and format information provided by clients**
   - Type client-dictated content verbatim
   - Format documents for professional appearance
   - Organize information logically
   - Create consistent formatting

2. **Provide blank standard forms and templates**
   - Offer blank templates for permitted document types
   - Direct clients to publicly available forms
   - Organize form collections by topic

3. **Provide general educational information**
   - Share general legal information (not advice)
   - Explain general procedures
   - Provide educational resources
   - Distribute publicly available guides

4. **Help with grammar, spelling, and clarity**
   - Proofread for typographical errors
   - Suggest grammar improvements
   - Enhance readability
   - Fix formatting issues

5. **Provide administrative support**
   - Help with organization of information
   - Assist with gathering publicly available records
   - Manage document version control
   - Maintain client files

### Prohibited Services

As a non-attorney document preparer in Minnesota and North Dakota, you **MUST NOT**:

1. **Give legal advice**
   - Analyze how laws apply to specific situations
   - Recommend legal courses of action
   - Interpret laws or regulations
   - Predict legal outcomes
   - Explain legal rights in specific circumstances

2. **Select which forms or documents a client needs**
   - Determine which document addresses a situation
   - Advise on which court forms to use
   - Choose between alternative legal documents
   - Create customized legal forms

3. **Prepare legal documents**
   - Draft court filings or pleadings
   - Prepare contracts or agreements
   - Create wills, trusts, or powers of attorney
   - Draft demand letters making legal claims

4. **Represent clients**
   - Appear in court
   - Negotiate on behalf of clients
   - Communicate with opposing parties
   - Sign documents on behalf of others

5. **Interpret legal documents**
   - Explain legal effects of document provisions
   - Analyze legal implications
   - Determine legal validity of documents
   - Interpret statutory requirements

## Required Disclaimers

All document preparation services must include clear disclaimers on:
- All marketing materials
- Service agreements
- Client communications
- Every document prepared

**Required standard disclaimers:**

```
I am not an attorney and cannot provide legal advice.
This document is not a substitute for legal advice.
This is not a legal document and has not been prepared or reviewed by an attorney.
I cannot represent you in court or in legal matters.
```

## Fee Structure Guidelines

Non-attorney document preparers **MAY** charge fees for:
- Typing and formatting services
- Document organization
- Administrative support
- Proofreading and editing
- General information provision

Non-attorney document preparers **MUST NOT** charge fees for:
- Legal advice
- Legal research
- Legal document selection
- Legal strategy development
- Legal representation
- Outcome-based fees (contingency fees)
